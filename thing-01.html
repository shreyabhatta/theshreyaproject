Take a solo trip to a city I've never been to

Just me, a bag, and no plan beyond the ticket.

Why this one

I'd been saying I'd do a solo trip for two years. Every time something would come up — a trip with family, a work deadline, a friend who "might also come." The "might" is the enemy of the solo trip. You can't plan around a might.

This time I just bought the ticket first. The decision made itself after that.

"The first evening alone in a city you've never seen is one of the purest feelings I know now. Everything is slightly off. You have to pay attention."

How it went

I went to Pondicherry. Took the overnight train. Arrived at 5am to a city that smells like coffee and salt and marigolds. I had a list of things I wanted to see and I ignored most of it — which turned out to be the right call.

The French Quarter is genuinely lovely in the early morning before the tourists arrive. I sat in a café for two hours and read. Talked to nobody. Then talked to the café owner for an hour. That was the best part.

I walked everywhere. Got very lost on day two (no data, on purpose). Found a bookshop I wouldn't have found otherwise. Bought four books I couldn't justify and read one of them on the train back.

What I learned

Traveling alone is mostly about the quality of your own company. It's a good diagnostic. I was fine — better than fine — which told me something. The loneliness that arrives around day 2 evening is its own kind of information. Not bad information.

I'd do it again immediately. I'm planning the next one — probably a longer trip, maybe abroad. The German I'm learning might come in useful.

Things I'd tell myself

Don't make a list of things you "must" see. Make a list of things you're curious about.

Eat at the counter, not the table. You'll talk to more people.

Turn your data off for at least one morning. It's not as scary as it sounds.
